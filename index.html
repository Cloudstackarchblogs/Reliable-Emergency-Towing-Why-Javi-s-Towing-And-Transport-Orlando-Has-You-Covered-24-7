What to Do When You Need Emergency Towing

Prioritize Your Safety

  • If possible, move your vehicle to the side of the road or a safe location to reduce the risk of further accidents.
  • Turn on your hazard lights to make your vehicle more visible to other drivers.
  • Use reflective triangles or flares, if available, to mark your position.

Stay Calm and Alert

  • Remain calm, even if you feel frustrated or anxious.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings.
  • If you’re in an unfamiliar or unsafe area, remain in your vehicle with the doors locked while waiting for assistance.

Tips to Avoid Roadside Emergencies

We are always ready to assist, taking preventive measures can reduce the likelihood of needing emergency towing. Here are some tips to help you avoid roadside breakdowns:

  • Regular Vehicle Maintenance: Scheduling regular maintenance helps prevent unexpected breakdowns. Routine services such as oil changes, brake inspections, and fluid checks keep your vehicle in good working condition.
  • Check Your Tires: Worn-out or underinflated tires increase the risk of blowouts. Before long trips, inspect your tire pressure and tread depth. Replace tires that show signs of excessive wear.
  • Keep an Emergency Kit: Having an emergency kit in your vehicle can make a big difference. Include essentials such as a flashlight, jumper cables, basic tools, a first aid kit, and water. Being prepared can help you manage minor issues while waiting for professional assistance.
